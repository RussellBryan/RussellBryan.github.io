Coffee Roaster

My obsession with coffee includes roasting it. Its also a great way to save money as I can roast coffee to similar quality as goes for $15 a pound for more like $6 a pound. Until recently, I was using a whirly-pop stove top pocorn popper as a roaster. It works well but is a little flimsy. I stumbled upon this much more durable one at a thrift store for $4. I bought a $10 120 RPM motor on amazon and a few spacers, a mount, and an adapter later, it was no longer hand crank. It works great, I plan on soon adding a thermocouple for increased acuracy when roasting.
