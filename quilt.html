Down Backpacking Quilt

After making my tent, the next piece of gear I wanted to upgrade was my sleeping bag. I wanted an to make a down quilt as lite as I could. I wanted something similar the the quilts sold by this company. I found I could buy the fabric I needed from thru-hiker.com for about $50. The best price for down I could find was from wildernesslogistics.com The measurements I took led me to believe I could get away 11 ounces of down which ran me about $90. The sewing went well. I found the straight seams easy after sewing all the complex curves for my tent.

I tested the quilt on a backpacking trip in Lassen Volcanic National Park. It turned out that I had sized the bag a bit too tightly. It worked, and kept me warm at an outside temperature under 40° F, however in order the maintain the warmth, I couldn't move at all. I was trying to make the bag as tight as I could in order to make the lightest bag possible, in hind sight, a few extra ounces would have been well worth the comfort. The bag works perfect as a warm weather bag though. Its perfectly fine for temperatures above 50° F and lighter than anything I could have bought. I'm considering getting into hammock backpacking and this would make a perfect top quilt.
